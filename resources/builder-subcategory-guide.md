# Builder — Subcategory Guide

## How to use this guide

When verifying a builder's service offerings, use this guide to understand what each subcategory actually means in practice. This helps you ask smarter questions, spot gaps, and understand why a builder might or might not offer certain services.

---

## Subcategory breakdown

| Subcategory | What it means in practice | Equipment/skill notes |
|-------------|---------------------------|----------------------|
| **General Building** | Catch-all for standard building work — repairs, maintenance, small builds | Most builders offer this as their base service |
| **New Home Builders** | Full new home construction — slab to handover | Major scope — requires full builder's licence, insurance, project management |
| **Home Renovations** | Reworking existing homes — kitchens, bathrooms, living areas, structural changes | Most common residential work — involves coordination of multiple trades |
| **Home Extensions** | Adding rooms, second storeys, granny flat conversions | Structural work — engineering, council approvals, often multi-month |
| **Granny Flat** | Self-contained secondary dwellings — prefab or custom build | Popular in NSW/VIC/QLD — specific council rules, CDC vs DA |
| **Kitchen Renovations** | Full kitchen remodels — cabinetry, benchtops, plumbing, electrical coordination | High-value reno — often the most requested builder service |
| **Garages, Sheds and Carports** | Standalone structures — garages, carports, garden sheds, workshops | Simpler scope than house builds, but still needs approvals above certain sizes |
| **Verandahs** | Covered outdoor areas — verandahs, balconies, alfresco areas | Crossover with carpentry — may involve roofing, concrete, steelwork |
| **Walls & Cladding** | External/internal wall construction, cladding installation and repair | Weatherboard, brick veneer, render, Hebel, composite panels |
| **Stumps** | Restumping, reblocking, subfloor repairs | Common in VIC/QLD — older homes on stumps need periodic replacement |
| **Drainage & Sewers** | Building-related drainage work — stormwater, site drainage, sewer connections | Distinct from plumbing — this is civil/structural drainage for building sites |
| **Fixtures & Fittings** | Installing doors, windows, architraves, skirting, built-in fixtures | Finishing work — often last stage of a build or reno |
| **Cool Rooms & Cellars** | Wine cellars, cool rooms, temperature-controlled storage | Specialist niche — often commercial (restaurants, florists) |
| **Building Supplies** | Supply of building materials | Some builders offer supply-only or supply-and-install |
| **Project Management** | Overseeing builds on behalf of owners — coordinating trades, timelines, budgets | Owner-builder support, commercial oversight |
| **Building Designs** | Drafting plans and designs for building projects | Crossover with Building Designer category — some builders do their own designs |
| **Cost Planning and Estimation** | Detailed cost estimates, quantity surveying, budget planning | Pre-build service — helps owners understand costs before committing |
| **Soil Testing** | Geotechnical soil assessment for foundations | Usually subcontracted — required before slab/footing design |

## Common patterns

**General Building** is the baseline — almost every builder offers this. If they've only selected this, probe whether they do new builds, renovations, or both.

**New Home Builders vs Renovators** is the key split. Some builders only do new builds (ground-up), others only do renovations/extensions, and some do both. New home builders need higher licence classes and insurance coverage.

**Kitchen Renovations and Home Renovations** are the bread and butter for most residential builders. If they've selected Home Renovations but not Kitchen Renovations, ask — almost every reno builder does kitchens.

**Home Extensions and Granny Flats** are structural work requiring engineering and council approvals. Not every renovator takes these on — they're more complex than internal renos.

**Stumps (restumping/reblocking)** is specialist work common in VIC and QLD where older homes sit on timber stumps. If they're in Melbourne or Brisbane, worth asking about.

**Project Management** is a different service model — the builder oversees but may not do the physical work. Some builders offer this alongside hands-on building; others are pure PM.

## Gaps to watch for

| If they've selected... | But not... | Ask about... |
|------------------------|------------|--------------|
| Home Renovations | Kitchen Renovations | Whether they do kitchen renos (most do) |
| Home Renovations | Home Extensions | Whether they take on extensions/second storeys |
| New Home Builders | Granny Flat | Whether they do granny flats (different scale but similar skills) |
| General Building | Garages, Sheds and Carports | Whether they build standalone structures |
| Home Renovations | Verandahs | Whether they do outdoor covered areas |
| General Building (VIC/QLD) | Stumps | Whether they do restumping work |
| Any building work | Project Management | Whether they offer PM-only services |
