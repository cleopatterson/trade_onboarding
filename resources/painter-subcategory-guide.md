# Painter — Subcategory Guide

## How to use this guide

When verifying a painter's service offerings, use this guide to understand what each subcategory actually means in practice. This helps you ask smarter questions, spot gaps, and understand why a painter might or might not offer certain services.

---

## Subcategory breakdown

| Subcategory | What it means in practice | Equipment/skill notes |
|-------------|---------------------------|----------------------|
| **House Painting** | General house painting — the catch-all | Most painters offer this as their base service |
| **Interior House Painting** | Painting inside — walls, ceilings, trim, doors | Standard domestic work, the bread and butter |
| **Exterior House Painting** | Painting outside — walls, eaves, fascia, window frames | Requires scaffolding/ladder access, weather dependent |
| **Fence Painting** | Painting or staining fences | Common add-on — timber fences, colourbond touch-ups |
| **Roof Painting** | Painting roofs — tile, colourbond, concrete | Specialist — requires roof access, proper coatings, safety equipment |
| **Wallpapering** | Hanging wallpaper, feature walls | Distinct skill — not all painters do wallpaper |
| **Commercial Painting** | Painting offices, warehouses, shops, strata | Larger scale, often after-hours, different pricing model |
| **Paint Removal** | Stripping old paint — chemical, sanding, heat gun | Lead paint removal is specialist work with safety requirements |
| **Floor Painting** | Painting floors — garage, warehouse, patio | Requires specific floor paints/epoxy coatings |
| **Concrete Painting** | Painting concrete surfaces — driveways, paths, walls | Similar to floor painting — specific coatings needed |

---

## Common patterns

**House Painting + Interior + Exterior** are the three baseline categories. Most painters offer all three. "House Painting" is the generic catch-all, while Interior and Exterior are more specific.

**Roof Painting** is specialist work. It requires working at height, specific coatings (roof membranes, heat-reflective paints), and often includes roof repairs/cleaning as part of the job. Many general painters don't do roofs.

**Wallpapering** is a distinct skill. Some painters are excellent wallpaperers; others won't touch it. If they haven't selected it, don't assume they do it.

**Commercial Painting** is a different market. It involves larger projects, different insurance, often after-hours work, and strata/body corporate relationships. Many residential painters don't do commercial.

**Floor and Concrete Painting** are related — both involve horizontal surfaces with specific coatings (epoxy, polyurethane). Garage floor epoxy is a growing niche.

**Paint Removal** can involve lead paint, which has strict safety requirements in pre-1970s homes. Painters who offer this should know the regulations.

## Gaps to watch for

| If they've selected... | But not... | Ask about... |
|------------------------|------------|--------------|
| Interior House Painting | Exterior House Painting | Whether they do exterior work (some only do interior) |
| House Painting | Roof Painting | Whether they do roof painting (specialist work) |
| House Painting | Wallpapering | Whether they hang wallpaper (distinct skill) |
| House Painting | Commercial Painting | Whether they do commercial/strata work |
| House Painting | Floor Painting | Whether they do garage floors/epoxy (growing niche) |
| Interior/Exterior | Paint Removal | Whether they do prep/stripping work (especially lead paint) |
| House Painting | Concrete Painting | Whether they paint concrete surfaces |
