# Upholsterer — Subcategory Guide

## How to use this guide

When verifying an upholsterer's service offerings, use this guide to understand what each subcategory actually means in practice. This helps you ask smarter questions, spot gaps, and understand why an upholsterer might or might not offer certain services.

---

## Subcategory breakdown

| Subcategory | What it means in practice | Equipment/skill notes |
|-------------|---------------------------|----------------------|
| **Upholstery Repair** | General upholstery repair — the catch-all | Most upholsterers offer this as their base service |
| **Lounge Suites** | Re-upholstering sofas and lounge suites | Core work — fabric replacement, foam, springs, webbing |
| **Dining Chairs** | Re-upholstering dining chairs — seats and backs | High-volume work — often done in sets of 6-12 |
| **Recliners** | Repairing and re-upholstering recliners | More complex — mechanisms, multiple moving parts |
| **Sofa Beds** | Repairing and re-upholstering sofa beds | Complex — fold-out mechanism plus upholstery |
| **Wingback Chairs** | Re-upholstering wingback/accent chairs | Traditional piece — requires careful pattern matching |
| **Bar Stools** | Re-upholstering bar stool seats | Simpler jobs but common — commercial and residential |
| **Bedheads** | Making or re-upholstering bed headboards | Custom work — popular for bedroom makeovers |
| **Ottomans** | Re-upholstering ottomans and footstools | Simpler pieces, often matched with lounge suites |
| **Antique Restoration** | Restoring antique furniture upholstery | Specialist — requires traditional techniques, heritage fabric sourcing |
| **Timber re-polishing** | Polishing and restoring timber frames on upholstered furniture | Complementary skill — not all upholsterers do timber work |
| **Outdoor cushions** | Making or re-covering outdoor furniture cushions | Requires weather-resistant fabrics (Sunbrella etc.) |
| **Custom made furniture** | Building new upholstered furniture from scratch | Highest skill level — design, frame building, full upholstery |
| **Leather Cleaning and Repair** | Cleaning, repairing, and re-dyeing leather furniture | Specialist — different skills and products from fabric upholstery |

---

## Common patterns

**Upholstery Repair + Lounge Suites + Dining Chairs + Recliners** are the four core services. Most upholsterers do all of these — they represent the bulk of residential upholstery work.

**Seating types** (Wingback, Bar Stools, Sofa Beds, Ottomans, Bedheads) are variations on the core skill. Most upholsterers who do lounge suites can handle these, but some specialise in certain types.

**Antique Restoration** is specialist work requiring knowledge of traditional techniques — hand-stitching, horsehair padding, period-correct fabric selection. Not all modern upholsterers have these skills.

**Leather** is a completely different material from fabric. Leather Cleaning and Repair requires different products, techniques, and equipment. Many fabric upholsterers don't work with leather.

**Custom made furniture** is the highest skill level — it involves designing and building new pieces from scratch, not just re-covering existing ones. Only experienced upholsterers offer this.

**Timber re-polishing** is a complementary skill. When re-upholstering a chair with exposed timber (dining chairs, wingbacks), the timber often needs refinishing too. Some upholsterers do this in-house; others outsource it.

**Outdoor cushions** require specific UV-resistant, water-resistant fabrics. It's a distinct market from indoor upholstery.

## Gaps to watch for

| If they've selected... | But not... | Ask about... |
|------------------------|------------|--------------|
| Lounge Suites | Recliners | Whether they handle recliner mechanisms (more complex) |
| Fabric upholstery | Leather Cleaning and Repair | Whether they work with leather (different specialty) |
| Upholstery Repair | Antique Restoration | Whether they do antique/heritage work (specialist skills) |
| Re-upholstery work | Custom made furniture | Whether they build new pieces or just recover existing |
| Indoor furniture | Outdoor cushions | Whether they do outdoor furniture (different fabrics) |
| Chair/sofa work | Timber re-polishing | Whether they refinish timber frames as well |
