# Kitchen Renovation Company — Subcategory Guide

## How to use this guide

When verifying a kitchen renovator's service offerings, use this guide to understand what each subcategory actually means in practice. This helps you ask smarter questions, spot gaps, and understand why a kitchen renovator might or might not offer certain services.

---

## Subcategory breakdown

| Subcategory | What it means in practice | Equipment/skill notes |
|-------------|---------------------------|----------------------|
| **Kitchen cabinet installation** | Installing kitchen cabinets — wall and base units | Core work — requires precise measurement, levelling |
| **Kitchen Benchtops** | Supplying and installing benchtops — stone, laminate, timber | Often coordinated with templating for stone tops |
| **Kitchen sink installation** | Installing kitchen sinks — undermount, top-mount, butler's | Requires plumbing coordination for waste/water |
| **Kitchen Tiling** | Tiling splashbacks, floors | Core finishing work — often subway tile, mosaic patterns |
| **Custom cabinets** | Designing and building custom cabinetry | Premium service — joinery skills, workshop required |
| **Flat pack kitchen installation** | Installing IKEA, Kaboodle, or other flat-pack kitchens | Budget option — assembly and fitting of pre-made components |
| **New kitchen planning** | Design services — layout, material selection, quoting | Full-service companies offer design; install-only companies don't |
| **Budget kitchen renovation** | Cost-effective kitchen updates — resurfacing, partial reno | Growing market — reface rather than replace |
| **Kitchen backsplash** | Installing splashback — tile, glass, acrylic, stone | Popular feature element — many material options |
| **Kitchen flooring** | Installing kitchen-specific flooring — vinyl, tile, timber | Needs to be moisture and spill resistant |

---

## Common patterns

**Cabinet installation + Benchtops + Sink + Tiling** are the four core services. A complete kitchen renovator handles all of these as part of a full kitchen reno.

**Custom cabinets vs Flat pack installation** is a key distinction. Custom cabinet makers design and build bespoke cabinetry in a workshop. Flat pack installers assemble pre-made kitchens (IKEA, Kaboodle, Bunnings). Some companies do both; many specialise in one or the other.

**New kitchen planning** separates full-service renovators from install-only companies. Design-inclusive companies handle everything from layout to material selection. Others expect the customer to arrive with a plan or use a third-party designer.

**Budget kitchen renovation** is a growing niche — resurfacing existing cabinets, replacing doors only, partial updates rather than full gut-and-replace. It's a different market from premium custom kitchens.

**Kitchen backsplash and flooring** are finishing elements that complete the kitchen. Not all renovators include flooring in their scope — it may be handled by a separate flooring company.

## Gaps to watch for

| If they've selected... | But not... | Ask about... |
|------------------------|------------|--------------|
| Kitchen cabinet installation | Custom cabinets | Whether they do custom joinery or just install pre-made |
| Kitchen cabinet installation | Flat pack kitchen installation | Whether they install flat-pack kitchens (different market) |
| Cabinet + benchtop work | New kitchen planning | Whether they offer design services or just installation |
| Full kitchen renovations | Budget kitchen renovation | Whether they do partial/budget renos (resurfacing, door replacement) |
| Tiling | Kitchen backsplash | Whether they do splashback specifically (popular standalone job) |
| Cabinet + benchtop | Kitchen flooring | Whether kitchen flooring is included in their scope |
