# Bathroom Renovation Company — Subcategory Guide

## How to use this guide

When verifying a bathroom renovator's service offerings, use this guide to understand what each subcategory actually means in practice. This helps you ask smarter questions, spot gaps, and understand why a bathroom renovator might or might not offer certain services.

---

## Subcategory breakdown

| Subcategory | What it means in practice | Equipment/skill notes |
|-------------|---------------------------|----------------------|
| **Bathroom Renovations** | Full bathroom renovations — the catch-all | Most bathroom reno companies offer this as their base service |
| **Bathroom Tiling** | Tiling floors, walls, and shower recesses | Core skill — waterproofing awareness essential |
| **Bathroom vanity installation** | Installing vanity units — wall-hung or freestanding | Requires plumbing coordination for waste/water connections |
| **Bathroom tap installation** | Installing or replacing taps and mixers | Often done alongside vanity or basin work |
| **Bathtub Resurfacing** | Re-enamelling or resurfacing existing bathtubs | Specialist — cheaper alternative to replacement |
| **Bathroom design** | Design services — layout, 3D renders, material selection | Not all renovators offer design — some are install-only |
| **Bathtub installation** | Installing new bathtubs — freestanding, built-in, corner | Involves plumbing, sometimes structural support |
| **Bathroom ceilings** | Ceiling work — moisture-resistant lining, exhaust fan installs | Often overlooked — moisture damage is common in bathrooms |
| **Bathroom lighting** | Installing bathroom lights — downlights, vanity lights, LED strips | Requires electrician coordination, IP-rated fittings |

---

## Common patterns

**Bathroom Renovations + Tiling + Vanity + Tap installation** are the four core services. A full bathroom renovator typically handles all of these as part of a complete reno.

**Bathroom design** separates full-service renovators from install-only companies. Companies offering design provide a more premium, end-to-end experience — layout planning, material selection, 3D renders. Others expect the customer to come with a plan.

**Bathtub Resurfacing** is a specialist restoration service — an alternative to ripping out and replacing an old bath. Not all renovators offer this; some are dedicated resurfacing businesses.

**Bathtub installation** is part of most full renovations but worth confirming — freestanding baths in particular may need structural considerations and specific plumbing.

**Bathroom ceilings and lighting** are finishing touches that complete a renovation. Ceiling work involves moisture-resistant materials; lighting requires an electrician. Companies that offer these provide a more complete package.

## Gaps to watch for

| If they've selected... | But not... | Ask about... |
|------------------------|------------|--------------|
| Bathroom Renovations | Bathroom design | Whether they offer design services or just installation |
| Bathroom Renovations | Bathtub Resurfacing | Whether they do resurfacing (often a separate specialty) |
| Bathroom Renovations | Bathroom ceilings | Whether they do ceiling work as part of renos |
| Bathroom Renovations | Bathroom lighting | Whether they coordinate electrical/lighting as part of renos |
| Tiling + Vanity | Bathtub installation | Whether they install bathtubs as well |
