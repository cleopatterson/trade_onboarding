# Concreter — Subcategory Guide

## How to use this guide

When verifying a concreter's service offerings, use this guide to understand what each subcategory actually means in practice. This helps you ask smarter questions, spot gaps, and understand why a concreter might or might not offer certain services.

---

## Subcategory breakdown

| Subcategory | What it means in practice | Equipment/skill notes |
|-------------|---------------------------|----------------------|
| **Concreting** | General concrete work — the catch-all | Most concreters offer this as their base service |
| **Concrete Slabs** | Pouring slabs — house, shed, garage foundations | Core work — requires formwork, reinforcement, finishing |
| **Concrete driveways & paths** | Pouring driveways, pathways, footpaths | Very common residential work |
| **Foundations** | House and building foundations | Structural work — engineering specs required |
| **Concrete Footpaths** | Public and private footpaths | Standard residential/commercial work |
| **Concrete Repair** | Fixing cracks, spalling, damaged concrete | Maintenance work — patching, resurfacing |
| **Concrete Removal** | Breaking up and removing old concrete | Requires jackhammer/bobcat, skip bin, disposal |
| **Concrete Formwork** | Building temporary forms for concrete pours | Skilled work — essential part of most concrete jobs |
| **Concrete Foundations** | Specifically foundations (vs general slabs) | Structural — strip footings, pad footings, piers |
| **Concrete Retaining Walls** | Poured concrete retaining walls | Structural — engineering often required |
| **Polished Concrete** | Grinding and polishing concrete floors to a shine | Specialist — requires diamond grinding equipment, sealers |
| **Decorative Concreting** | Stamped, stencilled, patterned concrete | Specialist finishing — requires skill and tooling |
| **Exposed Aggregate Concrete** | Concrete with exposed stone/pebble surface | Popular driveway/path finish — wash-off technique |
| **Coloured Concrete** | Concrete with integral colour or surface colour | Oxide additives or surface-applied colour |
| **Spray On Concreting** | Shotcrete/gunite — sprayed concrete | Specialist equipment — used for pools, retaining walls, slopes |
| **Concrete Flooring** | Indoor concrete floors — warehouse, residential | May overlap with polished concrete |
| **Concrete Paving** | Concrete pavers — stepping stones, patio pavers | Different from poured concrete — prefab units |
| **Concrete Sealing** | Applying sealant to protect concrete surfaces | Maintenance service — extends concrete life |
| **Concrete Grinding** | Grinding concrete to level or smooth surface | Prep work or standalone levelling service |
| **Concrete Cutting** | Saw-cutting concrete — expansion joints, trenches | Requires concrete saw — specialist equipment |
| **Concrete Core Drilling** | Drilling precise holes through concrete | Plumbing/electrical penetrations — specialist drill |
| **Concrete Edging** | Decorative or functional garden/path edging | Smaller-scale work — mow strips, borders |
| **Acrylic Rendering** | Applying acrylic render to walls/surfaces | Finishing trade — overlaps with rendering |
| **Bagging** | Bag-and-paint finish on brick/block walls | Finishing technique — cement slurry application |
| **Car Park Kerb Installation** | Installing concrete kerbs and wheel stops | Commercial work — car parks, driveways |
| **Bollards** | Installing concrete bollards for safety/access | Commercial/industrial — safety barriers |
| **Concrete Cladding** | Precast concrete panels for walls | Larger commercial work — precast installation |
| **Concrete Fencing** | Precast concrete fencing panels | Residential/commercial boundary fencing |
| **Concrete Resurfacing** | Overlaying existing concrete with new surface | Restoration — cheaper than full replacement |
| **Resealing Concrete** | Re-applying sealant to existing sealed concrete | Maintenance — periodic re-application |
| **Recolour Concrete** | Restoring or changing colour of existing concrete | Specialist restoration — staining, colour restore |
| **Concrete Restoration** | Full restoration of damaged/aged concrete | Comprehensive repair — may include resurfacing, resealing |
| **Driveway Restoration** | Specifically restoring driveways | Common residential service — repair + reseal + recolour |

## Common patterns

**Concreting + Slabs + Driveways + Foundations** are the baseline. Most concreters do all of these — they're bread-and-butter residential concrete work.

**Decorative finishes** (Polished, Decorative, Exposed Aggregate, Coloured, Spray On) are specialist skills. Not every concreter does decorative work. Polished concrete in particular requires expensive diamond grinding equipment and specific expertise.

**Exposed Aggregate** is extremely popular in Australia for driveways and paths. Many concreters offer it, but it requires good timing and technique for the wash-off process.

**Concrete Removal** is demolition work — jackhammers, bobcats, skip bins. Some concreters do removal as part of replacement jobs; others subcontract it.

**Rendering and Bagging** overlap with the rendering trade. Concreters who also render are covering wall finishing as well as flatwork.

**Commercial work** (Car Park Kerbs, Bollards, Concrete Cladding) is a different scale from residential. Requires larger equipment, different insurance, and often involves working with builders/developers.

**Restoration services** (Resurfacing, Resealing, Recolour, Restoration, Driveway Restoration) are growing — homeowners want to refresh existing concrete rather than rip up and replace. This is maintenance/restoration rather than new construction.

## Gaps to watch for

| If they've selected... | But not... | Ask about... |
|------------------------|------------|--------------|
| Concreting (general) | Polished Concrete | Whether they do polished/decorative finishes |
| Concreting (general) | Exposed Aggregate | Whether they do exposed aggregate (very popular) |
| Slabs & driveways | Concrete Retaining Walls | Whether they do structural retaining walls |
| Concreting (general) | Concrete Removal | Whether they do demolition/removal as well as new work |
| New concrete work | Concrete Repair/Restoration | Whether they do repair and maintenance work |
| Flatwork (slabs, driveways) | Concrete Cutting/Core Drilling | Whether they do cutting and drilling (often subcontracted) |
| Residential work | Car Park Kerb/Bollards | Whether they do commercial concrete work |
| General concrete | Acrylic Rendering/Bagging | Whether they also do wall finishing/rendering |
