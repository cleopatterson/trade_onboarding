# Subcategory Mapping Rules

## Overview

Most SS subcategories exist for SEO purposes. Real jobs typically come through under the **general heading** for each trade (e.g. "General Plumbing" rather than "Leaking taps"). Only a subset of subcategories represent genuinely distinct services that get their own job flow.

This document defines which subcategories are "general" (auto-tagged silently) vs "specialist" (worth asking the tradie about).

## How It Works

| Layer | What happens | User sees |
|-------|-------------|-----------|
| **General** | Auto-tagged in background | "Got you down for General Plumbing" |
| **Specialist** | Asked about in clusters | "Do you also do gas fitting?" |
| **Cross-category** | Suggested from co-occurrence data | "Most plumbers also list under Bathroom Renovation" |

The tradie never sees the individual general subcategories. They just get told the general heading and we tag them with all the standard subcats behind the scenes.
